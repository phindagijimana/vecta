Case 1: Epilepsy - Focal Seizure

Chief Complaint: Recurrent seizures with loss of awareness

Medications: Levetiracetam 500mg BID
MRI: Mesial temporal sclerosis
Classification: Focal epilepsy with impaired awareness
Seizure Type: Complex partial seizures

Case 2: Parkinson's Disease
Patient: 67-year-old male
Chief Complaint: Progressive tremor and slowness of movement
History: Symptoms started 3 years ago with right-hand tremor
Symptoms: Resting tremor, bradykinesia, rigidity, postural instability
UPDRS Score: 42 (moderate severity)
Medications: Carbidopa-Levodopa 25-100mg TID
Response to Treatment: Good initial response, wearing-off after 4 hours
Examination: Masked facies, cogwheel rigidity, reduced arm swing
Diagnosis: Parkinson's Disease, Hoehn and Yahr Stage 2

Case 3: Stroke - Ischemic CVA
Patient: 72-year-old female
Chief Complaint: Sudden onset right-sided weakness and slurred speech
History: Hypertension, atrial fibrillation (not anticoagulated)
Symptoms: Right hemiparesis, dysarthria, facial droop
NIHSS Score: 14 (moderate-severe stroke)
CT Head: Hypodense area in left MCA territory
MRI: Diffusion restriction in left frontoparietal region
Time from Onset: 2.5 hours
Treatment: IV tPA administered, transferred for thrombectomy
Diagnosis: Acute ischemic stroke, left MCA territory

Case 4: Migraine with Aura
Patient: 34-year-old female
Chief Complaint: Severe recurrent headaches with visual disturbances
History: Monthly headaches since age 18, family history positive
Symptoms: Unilateral throbbing headache, photophobia, phonophobia, nausea
Aura: Zigzag lines, scotomas lasting 20-30 minutes before headache
Triggers: Stress, lack of sleep, hormonal changes
Duration: 12-24 hours per episode
Medications: Sumatriptan 100mg PRN, propranolol 80mg daily prophylaxis
Diagnosis: Migraine with typical aura, chronic migraine

Case 5: Multiple Sclerosis
Patient: 31-year-old female
Chief Complaint: Episodes of numbness and vision problems
History: Two prior episodes of optic neuritis
Symptoms: Bilateral leg numbness, fatigue, Lhermitte's sign positive
MRI Brain: Multiple periventricular and juxtacortical T2 hyperintensities
MRI Spine: Cervical cord lesion at C3-C4
CSF: Oligoclonal bands present, elevated IgG index
Visual Evoked Potentials: Prolonged P100 latency bilaterally
Diagnosis: Relapsing-Remitting Multiple Sclerosis
McDonald Criteria: Satisfied (dissemination in time and space)

Case 6: Alzheimer's Dementia
Patient: 76-year-old male
Chief Complaint: Progressive memory loss affecting daily activities
History: Gradual decline over 3 years, getting lost in familiar places
Symptoms: Short-term memory impairment, word-finding difficulties, apraxia
MMSE: 18/30 (moderate cognitive impairment)
MoCA: 14/30
MRI Brain: Hippocampal atrophy, generalized cortical atrophy
PET Scan: Reduced temporal and parietal metabolism
Biomarkers: Low CSF AÎ²42, elevated tau and p-tau
Diagnosis: Probable Alzheimer's Disease, moderate stage

Case 7: Peripheral Neuropathy
Patient: 58-year-old male
Chief Complaint: Burning feet and numbness in hands
History: Type 2 diabetes for 15 years, poor glycemic control
Symptoms: Distal symmetric paresthesias, burning pain worse at night
Examination: Decreased pinprick and vibration sense in stocking-glove distribution
Reflexes: Absent ankle jerks bilaterally
EMG/NCS: Reduced amplitude, slowed conduction velocities
HbA1c: 9.2%
Diagnosis: Diabetic polyneuropathy, distal symmetric sensorimotor type

Case 8: Myasthenia Gravis
Patient: 42-year-old female
Chief Complaint: Double vision and drooping eyelids
History: Symptoms worse in evening, improve with rest
Symptoms: Ptosis, diplopia, fatigue with prolonged activity
Examination: Fatigable ptosis, weakness of eye movements
Ice Pack Test: Positive (improvement of ptosis)
Edrophonium Test: Positive response
AChR Antibodies: Positive (elevated titer)
CT Chest: Thymic hyperplasia
EMG: Decremental response on repetitive nerve stimulation
Diagnosis: Ocular Myasthenia Gravis

Case 9: Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)
Patient: 61-year-old male
Chief Complaint: Progressive weakness and muscle wasting
History: Started with right hand weakness 8 months ago
Symptoms: Muscle weakness, fasciculations, dysarthria, dysphagia
Examination: Upper and lower motor neuron signs
    - UMN: Hyperreflexia, spasticity, positive Babinski sign
    - LMN: Atrophy, fasciculations, weakness
EMG: Widespread denervation and fasciculations
Respiratory: FVC 72% of predicted
Diagnosis: Probable ALS (El Escorial criteria)
Progression: Upper limb onset, bulbar involvement

Case 10: Cervical Spinal Cord Compression
Patient: 54-year-old male
Chief Complaint: Neck pain with progressive weakness in legs
History: Chronic neck pain, recent worsening with gait difficulty
Symptoms: Bilateral leg weakness, urinary urgency, hand clumsiness
Examination: Spastic paraparesis, hyperreflexia, upgoing toes
    - Sensory level at C5-C6
    - Positive Hoffman's sign bilaterally
MRI Cervical Spine: Large disc herniation at C5-C6
    - Severe spinal cord compression with T2 signal change
    - Cord compression 75%
Diagnosis: Cervical myelopathy due to disc herniation
Urgency: Surgical decompression recommended
