CASE 1: EPILEPSY CLASSIFICATION
Patient: 28-year-old female
Chief Complaint: Recurrent episodes of altered awareness

History: Patient reports 6-month history of episodes beginning with sudden epigastric rising sensation, followed by behavioral arrest, oral automatisms (lip smacking, chewing), and bilateral hand automatisms. Episodes last 1-2 minutes. Post-ictal confusion for 5-10 minutes. Occurs 2-3 times weekly. No tonic-clonic activity reported.

EEG Findings: Interictal left temporal sharp waves (F7/T3). Ictal recording shows rhythmic theta activity originating from left temporal region.

Imaging: MRI brain shows left hippocampal sclerosis with volume loss and increased T2 signal.

Medication Trial: Failed levetiracetam 1500mg BID (breakthrough seizures 2-3x/week).

Family History: Mother with epilepsy (unspecified type).
